## 1. Purpose

This FAQ provides guidance for employees working remotely or in hybrid roles regarding home office equipment, workspace setup, and company-provided resources. The goal is to help employees create a productive, comfortable, and secure work environment while working from home.

These guidelines apply to employees who regularly perform their duties outside the company office.

---

## 2. What Equipment Does the Company Provide?

The company provides essential equipment required to perform work duties effectively.

Standard equipment includes:

* Company-issued laptop
* Laptop charger
* Secure VPN access
* Collaboration software tools
* Security authentication tools

Depending on the role, employees may also receive:

* External monitor
* Keyboard and mouse
* Headset for meetings

All company-issued equipment remains company property and must be returned when employment ends.

---

## 3. Is There a Work From Home Setup Allowance?

Yes. Employees may receive a **one-time remote workspace setup allowance of $500**.

This allowance can be used to purchase items that improve productivity and ergonomics.

Examples of eligible purchases include:

* Ergonomic office chair
* Adjustable desk
* External monitor
* Desk lighting
* Laptop stand
* Keyboard and mouse

Employees must submit receipts to claim reimbursement.

---

## 4. Can I Purchase My Own Equipment?

Employees may purchase additional equipment if it supports their work setup. However, purchases exceeding the standard allowance must receive manager approval.

Examples requiring approval:

```id="u8g3jd"
High-end monitor  
Standing desk  
Specialized equipment
```

Employees should confirm approval before making purchases to ensure reimbursement eligibility.

---

## 5. What Internet Requirements Are Recommended?

Employees working remotely must maintain a stable internet connection.

Recommended internet speed:

```id="x1b6m2"
Minimum 50 Mbps download
Minimum 10 Mbps upload
```

Reliable internet connectivity ensures smooth video conferencing, file transfers, and remote system access.

Employees are responsible for maintaining their home internet service.

---

## 6. How Should I Set Up My Workspace?

Employees should create a workspace that promotes productivity and minimizes distractions.

Recommended workspace setup:

* Quiet environment for calls and meetings
* Comfortable chair with proper back support
* Desk at appropriate height
* Adequate lighting
* Secure place for company devices

Example ergonomic setup:

```id="o9p3zt"
Monitor at eye level  
Keyboard at elbow height  
Feet flat on the floor
```

This helps reduce strain and improve long-term comfort.

---

## 7. What Security Practices Must Be Followed?

Remote employees must follow company security policies to protect company data.

Security requirements include:

* Use company-issued devices for work
* Connect to company systems using VPN
* Enable multi-factor authentication
* Lock devices when not in use
* Avoid using public Wi-Fi without VPN

Sensitive company information should not be stored on personal devices.

---

## 8. What Should I Do If My Equipment Stops Working?

If company equipment experiences technical issues, employees should contact the IT support team immediately.

Steps to follow:

1. Submit a support request through the IT helpdesk
2. Provide a description of the issue
3. Include screenshots if possible

Example issue report:

```id="y6l1av"
Laptop unable to connect to VPN after system update.
```

IT may provide remote troubleshooting or arrange a replacement device if necessary.

---

## 9. Who Is Responsible for Equipment Maintenance?

Employees are responsible for taking reasonable care of company-issued equipment.

Employees should:

* Avoid physical damage
* Keep devices clean
* Install required security updates
* Use protective cases when transporting devices

Lost or damaged equipment should be reported immediately.

---

## 10. Can I Work From Locations Other Than My Home?

Employees may occasionally work from alternative locations such as coworking spaces or temporary residences.

However, employees must ensure:

* Reliable internet connectivity
* Secure working environment
* Compliance with company security policies

Employees should inform their manager if they plan to work from a different location for an extended period.

---

## 11. Are Office Resources Still Available?

Yes. Employees working remotely may still use office facilities when needed.

Office resources include:

* Meeting rooms
* Quiet workspaces
* Collaboration areas
* IT support assistance

Employees may reserve workspace using the company office booking system.

---

## 12. Who Can I Contact for Support?

Employees may contact the following teams for assistance:

| Team       | Support Provided           |
| ---------- | -------------------------- |
| IT Support | Technical issues           |
| HR         | Remote work policies       |
| Facilities | Office equipment questions |
| Manager    | Work arrangement approval  |

Employees should reach out promptly if they encounter challenges with their work-from-home setup.

---

## 13. Policy Updates

The company may update remote work and equipment guidelines as technology, workplace needs, and company policies evolve.

Employees will be notified of any updates through official HR communication channels.
