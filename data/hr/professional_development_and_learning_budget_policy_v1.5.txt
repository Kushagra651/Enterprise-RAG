## 1. Purpose

The company encourages continuous learning and professional development for all employees. This policy outlines the professional development benefits available to employees, including learning budgets, training opportunities, and reimbursement procedures.

The goal of this program is to support employees in expanding their skills, improving job performance, and advancing their careers while contributing to the company's growth.

---

## 2. Annual Learning Budget

Each full-time employee is eligible for an **annual professional development budget of $1,000**.

This budget may be used for learning activities such as:

* Online courses
* Professional certifications
* Technical workshops
* Industry conferences
* Educational books
* Skill development programs

Example eligible expense:

```id="b6y9l1"
Machine Learning certification course – $450
```

Unused learning budget **does not carry over** to the next calendar year.

---

## 3. Eligibility Requirements

Employees become eligible for the learning budget after completing:

```id="n4u7pr"
3 months of employment
```

Employees must also be in good standing with their performance reviews.

Contractors and temporary employees may not be eligible unless approved by their manager and HR.

---

## 4. Eligible Learning Activities

Employees may use their learning budget for activities that support professional growth and job-related skills.

Examples of eligible learning resources include:

* Technical courses (software development, data science, design)
* Leadership training programs
* Project management certifications
* Industry conferences or seminars
* Professional memberships
* Educational books or subscriptions

Example course purchase:

```id="t1e5m7"
Advanced React Development Course – $120
```

Managers are encouraged to discuss learning plans with employees during performance reviews.

---

## 5. Ineligible Expenses

The following expenses are not covered by the learning budget:

* Personal hobbies unrelated to work
* Academic degree programs (unless approved separately)
* Travel costs unrelated to training
* Hardware purchases such as laptops or tablets

Example non-eligible expense:

```id="h2x9vf"
Gaming course unrelated to job responsibilities
```

Employees should confirm eligibility before purchasing training resources.

---

## 6. Conference and Workshop Attendance

Employees may use their learning budget to attend professional conferences or workshops.

Typical expenses may include:

* Registration fees
* Online conference passes
* Workshop participation

For in-person conferences, travel costs may be reimbursed separately under the **Expense Reimbursement Policy**, subject to manager approval.

Example:

```id="g8p0jw"
Software Engineering Conference Registration – $350
```

Employees should discuss conference participation with their manager beforehand.

---

## 7. Approval Process

Employees must obtain manager approval before using their learning budget.

Approval steps:

1. Submit training request
2. Provide course details
3. Explain how the training supports job responsibilities
4. Receive manager approval

Example request message:

```id="s9q4yn"
Request approval for a Python Data Engineering course to improve data pipeline development skills.
```

Managers should review and approve requests within **3 business days**.

---

## 8. Reimbursement Process

After completing the learning activity, employees must submit a reimbursement request.

Required documentation includes:

* Proof of payment
* Invoice or receipt
* Description of course or training
* Completion certificate (if applicable)

Example reimbursement entry:

```id="w5j8bz"
Course: Cloud Architecture Fundamentals  
Cost: $220
```

Reimbursements are typically processed within:

```id="y3n0da"
10 business days
```

---

## 9. Knowledge Sharing

Employees are encouraged to share knowledge gained from training activities.

Examples include:

* Presenting learnings in team meetings
* Writing internal documentation
* Conducting short knowledge-sharing sessions

Example:

```id="c7r3lo"
Present summary of DevOps conference insights during team meeting.
```

This helps spread knowledge across teams and maximize the value of training investments.

---

## 10. Learning Goals

Employees are encouraged to set **1–2 professional development goals per year**.

Examples may include:

* Learning a new programming language
* Obtaining a professional certification
* Improving leadership skills
* Developing expertise in a specialized area

Managers may include these goals in performance development plans.

---

## 11. Policy Compliance

Employees must ensure that learning budget funds are used appropriately.

Misuse of the learning budget may result in:

* Reimbursement denial
* Policy review with HR
* Disciplinary action

Employees should retain receipts and documentation for all learning-related purchases.

---

## 12. Policy Updates

This policy may be reviewed periodically by HR to align with company growth and employee learning needs.

Any updates will be communicated through official HR announcements.
