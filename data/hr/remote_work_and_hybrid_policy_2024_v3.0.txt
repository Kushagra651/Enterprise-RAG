Title: Remote Work and Hybrid Policy 2024
Type: Policy
Department: HR
Effective Date: January 8, 2024
Version: v3.0
-------------

## 1. Purpose

This policy outlines the company's guidelines for remote work and hybrid work arrangements. The goal is to provide employees with flexibility while maintaining productivity, collaboration, and organizational effectiveness.

This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees unless their job role requires a fully on-site presence.

---

## 2. Work Model Overview

The company follows a **hybrid-first work model** designed to balance flexibility with in-person collaboration.

Employees may choose one of the following work arrangements based on team requirements and manager approval:

**Hybrid Work**

Employees work:

* **2–3 days per week in the office**
* **2–3 days per week remotely**

Typical hybrid schedule example:

```
Monday – Office  
Tuesday – Remote  
Wednesday – Office  
Thursday – Remote  
Friday – Remote
```

**Fully Remote**

Certain roles may be designated as fully remote depending on the team’s operational requirements.

Remote employees must still be available during core working hours.

---

## 3. Core Working Hours

All employees must be available during company core collaboration hours:

```
10:00 AM – 4:00 PM (local time)
```

Outside of core hours, employees may choose flexible working schedules as long as work responsibilities are met.

Managers may establish additional team-specific hours if required.

---

## 4. Eligibility

Remote and hybrid work eligibility depends on:

* Job role
* Team collaboration needs
* Performance history
* Manager approval

Roles that require physical presence (e.g., hardware operations, office support) may not qualify for full remote work.

Employees must maintain satisfactory performance to continue remote eligibility.

---

## 5. Remote Work Expectations

Employees working remotely must meet the following expectations:

* Maintain a reliable internet connection
* Be reachable through company communication tools (Slack, email, Zoom)
* Attend scheduled meetings
* Meet deadlines and performance expectations

Employees should maintain a **professional and distraction-free work environment**.

---

## 6. Equipment and Workspace

The company supports remote work by providing necessary equipment.

Standard remote work equipment includes:

* Company-issued laptop
* External monitor (optional)
* Keyboard and mouse
* VPN access
* Collaboration software tools

Employees may also receive a **$500 remote workspace setup stipend** when starting remote work.

This stipend may be used for:

* Office chairs
* Desk setup
* Lighting
* Ergonomic accessories

Equipment provided by the company remains company property.

---

## 7. Communication Requirements

Remote employees must remain actively engaged with their teams.

Expected communication practices:

* Daily updates in team Slack channels
* Participation in scheduled meetings
* Timely responses to messages (within reasonable working hours)

Recommended response expectations:

```
Slack messages: within 2 hours
Email: within 24 hours
```

Teams may also conduct **weekly check-ins** to ensure alignment and progress.

---

## 8. Office Usage

Employees working in a hybrid model may use company offices as needed.

Office resources include:

* Meeting rooms
* Collaboration spaces
* Quiet work areas
* High-speed internet
* IT support

Employees may reserve desks or meeting rooms using the office booking system.

---

## 9. Travel to Office

Employees living within **50 km of the office** may be expected to attend certain in-person meetings or team collaboration days.

Employees working remotely outside commuting distance may be required to travel for:

* Quarterly team meetings
* Company-wide events
* Strategic planning sessions

Travel expenses for these events will be covered according to the **Expense Reimbursement Policy**.

---

## 10. Security and Data Protection

Remote work must comply with company data security policies.

Employees must:

* Use company-issued devices
* Access company systems through VPN
* Avoid using public Wi-Fi without secure connections
* Lock screens when away from devices

Sensitive company data must never be stored on personal devices.

---

## 11. Performance Monitoring

Remote employees are evaluated based on:

* Work quality
* Meeting deadlines
* Collaboration
* Contribution to team goals

The company does **not track employee activity through surveillance tools**. Performance evaluation is based on outcomes and results.

---

## 12. Policy Violations

Failure to meet remote work expectations may result in:

* Adjustment of remote work privileges
* Requirement to work from office
* Performance review discussions

Managers may review remote work arrangements periodically.

---

## 13. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by HR and leadership to ensure it continues to meet the needs of the company and employees.

Updates will be communicated through the internal HR portal and company announcements.
