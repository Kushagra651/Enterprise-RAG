## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the guidelines for reimbursing employees for approved business-related expenses incurred while performing company duties. The goal is to ensure that employees are fairly reimbursed while maintaining financial accountability and transparency.

All employees must follow this policy when submitting expense reimbursement requests.

---

## 2. Eligible Business Expenses

Employees may be reimbursed for expenses that are **reasonable, necessary, and directly related to business activities**.

Common reimbursable expenses include:

* Business travel (flights, trains, taxis)
* Hotel accommodation for work travel
* Meals during business travel
* Client meetings
* Conference registration fees
* Work-related office supplies
* Remote work setup expenses (if approved)

Example:

```id="7n4a5p"
Taxi ride to client meeting – reimbursable
```

Expenses unrelated to business activities will not be reimbursed.

---

## 3. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following expenses are generally **not eligible for reimbursement**:

* Personal travel costs
* Personal meals not related to work
* Alcohol (unless approved for client events)
* Traffic fines or parking violations
* Personal entertainment
* Unapproved equipment purchases

Example:

```id="uxc2f8"
Movie tickets during travel – not reimbursable
```

Employees should consult their manager if they are unsure whether an expense qualifies.

---

## 4. Travel Expense Guidelines

Employees traveling for work must follow company travel guidelines.

### Air Travel

Standard policy:

```id="c0u1jp"
Economy class for flights under 6 hours
```

Business class travel may be approved for:

* Flights longer than **8 hours**
* Senior leadership travel

Flights should be booked at least **7 days in advance** whenever possible to minimize costs.

---

### Accommodation

Employees may book hotels with a nightly cost up to:

```id="m5f9ek"
$200 per night
```

Exceptions require manager approval.

Employees should choose reasonably priced accommodations close to the work location.

---

### Meal Expenses

Employees may claim meal expenses during business travel.

Daily meal reimbursement limit:

```id="l0v8sq"
$50 per day
```

This limit includes breakfast, lunch, and dinner.

Itemized receipts are required for all meal claims.

---

## 5. Remote Work Expenses

Employees working remotely may request reimbursement for approved home office equipment.

Annual home office reimbursement limit:

```id="p4b6zr"
$300 per year
```

Eligible items include:

* Ergonomic office chairs
* External monitors
* Desk accessories
* Webcams or headsets

Employees must receive manager approval before purchasing equipment.

---

## 6. Expense Submission Process

Employees must submit reimbursement requests through the company's expense management system.

Required steps:

1. Upload itemized receipts
2. Provide expense description
3. Select expense category
4. Submit for manager approval

Example expense description:

```id="v2n7xa"
Lunch meeting with client regarding project planning.
```

Receipts must clearly show:

* Vendor name
* Purchase date
* Amount paid
* Description of items

---

## 7. Submission Deadline

Expense reports must be submitted within:

```id="0q7jwr"
30 days of the expense date
```

Late submissions may be rejected unless an exception is approved by HR or Finance.

---

## 8. Approval Process

Expense claims must be reviewed and approved by the employee's direct manager.

For large expenses above:

```id="9u3tgc"
$500
```

Additional approval from the Finance department may be required.

Managers should review expense reports within **5 business days**.

---

## 9. Reimbursement Timeline

Once approved, reimbursements will be processed through payroll or direct bank transfer.

Typical reimbursement timeline:

```id="1m9bkp"
7–10 business days
```

Employees will receive a notification once reimbursement has been processed.

---

## 10. Company Credit Cards

Certain employees may be issued company credit cards for business expenses.

Company card usage must follow these rules:

* Only business-related purchases allowed
* Receipts must still be submitted
* Personal charges must be repaid immediately

Misuse of company cards may result in disciplinary action.

---

## 11. Compliance and Auditing

The Finance team may periodically review expense claims to ensure compliance with company policies.

Employees may be asked to provide additional documentation during audits.

Intentional misuse of the reimbursement policy may result in disciplinary action.

---

## 12. Policy Updates

This policy may be updated periodically based on financial policies and business requirements.

Employees will be notified of policy changes through official HR communications.
